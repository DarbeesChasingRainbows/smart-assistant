// First Aid Fundamentals & Emergency Response Deck

What is the primary purpose of first aid?
To provide immediate care to injured or ill individuals until professional medical help arrives, preserving life, preventing worsening of conditions, and promoting recovery.

What is the difference between first aid and professional medical care?
First aid provides immediate temporary care at the scene, while professional medical care provides comprehensive treatment in medical facilities.

What is the primary purpose of the DRABC acronym in emergency response?
To ensure systematic assessment: Danger, Response, Airway, Breathing, Circulation - the standard approach to emergency scene evaluation.

What is the difference between primary and secondary survey in first aid?
Primary survey quickly assesses life-threatening conditions using DRABC, while secondary survey performs detailed head-to-toe examination after immediate threats are managed.

What is the primary purpose of calling emergency services in first aid situations?
To activate professional medical response and provide critical information about location, number of patients, and nature of injuries for appropriate resource dispatch.

What is the difference between calling 911 and other emergency numbers?
911 is universal emergency number in North America, while other regions use different numbers (112 in Europe, 999 in UK) for emergency services.

What is the primary purpose of scene safety assessment in first aid?
To protect both rescuer and victim from ongoing dangers, ensuring emergency response can be provided safely without creating additional victims.

What is the difference between scene safety and personal protective equipment?
Scene safety assesses environmental hazards, while PPE provides protection from bloodborne pathogens and other contamination risks.

What is the primary purpose of consent in first aid?
To respect patient autonomy and legal requirements, obtaining permission before providing care except when patient is unconscious or in life-threatening situations.

What is the difference between expressed and implied consent in first aid?
Expressed consent is verbal or written permission, while implied consent applies to unconscious patients where it's assumed they would want help.

What is the primary purpose of good samaritan laws?
To protect rescuers from legal liability when providing reasonable assistance in good faith during emergencies, encouraging people to help others without fear of lawsuits.

What is the difference between good samaritan protection and negligence?
Good samaritan laws protect reasonable care efforts, while negligence involves care that falls below accepted standards causing harm.

What is the primary purpose of infection control in first aid?
To prevent transmission of diseases between rescuer and patient through proper hand hygiene, PPE use, and safe handling of body fluids.

What is the difference between standard precautions and transmission-based precautions?
Standard precautions apply to all patients, while transmission-based precautions are additional measures for specific known infections.

What is the primary purpose of hand hygiene in first aid?
To remove pathogens and prevent cross-contamination, the single most effective measure for infection control in emergency care.

What is the difference between hand washing and hand sanitizers?
Hand washing uses soap and water to remove all types of pathogens, while alcohol sanitizers kill most germs but don't remove dirt or all organisms.

What is the primary purpose of gloves in first aid?
To provide barrier protection against bloodborne pathogens and reduce contamination risk during patient care and wound management.

What is the difference between sterile and clean gloves?
Sterile gloves are free from all microorganisms for invasive procedures, while clean gloves reduce but don't eliminate contamination risk.

What is the primary purpose of CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)?
To maintain blood circulation and oxygenation to vital organs when heart and breathing have stopped, buying time until professional help arrives.

What is the difference between compression-only and full CPR?
Compression-only CPR provides chest compressions only for untrained rescuers, while full CPR includes both compressions and rescue breaths.

What is the primary purpose of chest compressions in CPR?
To manually pump blood through the body by compressing the heart between sternum and spine, maintaining circulation to brain and other vital organs.

What is the difference between adult and child CPR compression depth?
Adult compressions should be 2-2.4 inches deep, while child compressions should be about 2 inches deep, using appropriate force for body size.

What is the primary purpose of rescue breaths in CPR?
To provide oxygen to the lungs when natural breathing has stopped, supplying oxygen to blood being circulated by chest compressions.

What is the difference between mouth-to-mouth and barrier device rescue breaths?
Mouth-to-mouth provides direct breaths, while barrier devices (pocket masks) protect rescuer from direct contact while delivering breaths.

What is the primary purpose of AED (Automated External Defibrillator) use?
To deliver electrical shocks to restore normal heart rhythm in sudden cardiac arrest, significantly improving survival rates when used quickly.

What is the difference between AED and manual defibrillation?
AED automatically analyzes heart rhythm and advises shocks, while manual defibrillation requires professional interpretation of ECG rhythms.

What is the primary purpose of recovery position in first aid?
To maintain open airway for unconscious breathing patients and prevent choking, while keeping the person stable until help arrives.

What is the difference between recovery position and CPR?
Recovery position is for unconscious breathing patients, while CPR is for patients without breathing or pulse requiring immediate resuscitation.

What is the primary purpose of choking first aid?
To clear airway obstruction using abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) or chest thrusts, restoring breathing ability for conscious or unconscious victims.

What is the difference between mild and severe choking management?
Mild choking encourages coughing, while severe choking requires immediate intervention with thrusts to clear airway obstruction.

What is the primary purpose of bleeding control in first aid?
To stop or slow blood loss to prevent shock and preserve life, using direct pressure, elevation, and pressure points as needed.

What is the difference between arterial and venous bleeding?
Arterial bleeding spurts bright red blood and is life-threatening, while venous bleeding flows steadily dark red blood and is typically less severe.

What is the primary purpose of direct pressure for bleeding control?
To apply pressure directly to wound site to promote clotting and reduce blood flow, the first and most effective bleeding control method.

What is the difference between direct pressure and pressure points?
Direct pressure applies force to wound site, while pressure points apply pressure to arteries above wound to reduce blood flow to area.

What is the primary purpose of tourniquets in severe bleeding?
To completely stop blood flow to limb in life-threatening extremity bleeding when direct pressure fails, preventing fatal blood loss.

What is the difference between commercial and improvised tourniquets?
Commercial tourniquets are designed for effective, safe application, while improvised tourniquets use available materials but may be less effective.

What is the primary purpose of wound cleaning and dressing?
To prevent infection and promote healing by removing contaminants and providing protective barrier over injured tissue.

What is the difference between cleaning and disinfecting wounds?
Cleaning removes dirt and debris with water or saline, while disinfecting kills microorganisms using antiseptics.

What is the primary purpose of burn classification in first aid?
To determine severity and appropriate treatment: first-degree (superficial), second-degree (partial thickness), third-degree (full thickness) burns.

What is the difference between thermal and chemical burns?
Thermal burns from heat sources require cooling, while chemical burns require neutralization and removal of causative agent before cooling.

What is the primary purpose of burn first aid treatment?
To stop burning process, cool the burn, prevent infection, and reduce pain while seeking appropriate medical care based on burn severity.

What is the difference between minor and major burn management?
Minor burns can be treated with cooling and dressings on site, while major burns require immediate emergency medical care and specialized treatment.

What is the primary purpose of fracture and sprain management?
To immobilize injured area, prevent further damage, reduce pain, and maintain proper alignment until professional medical evaluation.

What is the difference between fractures and sprains?
Fractures involve broken bones requiring immobilization, while sprains involve ligament damage requiring RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation).

What is the primary purpose of splinting in first aid?
To immobilize injured limbs to prevent further damage, reduce pain, and maintain proper position during transport to medical care.

What is the difference between rigid and soft splints?
Rigid splints provide firm immobilization for suspected fractures, while soft splints offer flexible support for sprains and minor injuries.

What is the primary purpose of RICE therapy for soft tissue injuries?
To reduce pain and swelling through Rest, Ice, Compression, and Elevation, promoting healing and preventing further tissue damage.

What is the difference between ice and heat application in injuries?
Ice reduces swelling and pain in acute injuries (first 48 hours), while heat increases blood flow for healing in later stages or chronic conditions.

What is the primary purpose of head injury assessment in first aid?
To identify potentially serious brain injuries requiring immediate medical care, using neurological checks and monitoring for deterioration.

What is the difference between concussion and more serious head injuries?
Concussions involve temporary brain function disruption, while serious injuries may involve bleeding or swelling requiring immediate emergency care.

What is the primary purpose of spinal injury precautions?
To prevent further damage to spinal cord by immobilizing head and neck, preventing paralysis or death from improper movement.

What is the difference between suspected and confirmed spinal injuries?
Suspected injuries require full immobilization precautions, while confirmed injuries need specialized handling and emergency medical transport.

What is the primary purpose of diabetic emergency management?
To recognize and treat hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) or hyperglycemia (high blood sugar) emergencies, preventing life-threatening complications.

What is the difference between hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia symptoms?
Hypoglycemia causes shakiness, sweating, confusion with rapid onset, while hyperglycemia causes thirst, frequent urination with gradual onset.

What is the primary purpose of seizure first aid?
To protect person from injury during seizure, maintain airway, and provide appropriate post-seizure care while preventing complications.

What is the difference between seizure and fainting management?
Seizures require protecting from injury and timing the episode, while fainting requires airway protection and leg elevation after recovery.

What is the primary purpose of asthma attack first aid?
To help person use rescue inhaler properly, maintain calm breathing, and seek emergency care if symptoms don't improve or worsen.

What is the difference between rescue and controller inhalers?
Rescue inhalers provide quick relief during attacks, while controller inhalers prevent attacks when used regularly as prescribed.

What is the primary purpose of allergic reaction management?
To identify severity, administer appropriate treatment like antihistamines or epinephrine, and seek emergency care for severe reactions (anaphylaxis).

What is the difference between mild and severe allergic reactions?
Mild reactions cause localized symptoms treatable with antihistamines, while severe reactions (anaphylaxis) affect breathing and require epinephrine.

What is the primary purpose of epinephrine auto-injectors in anaphylaxis?
To rapidly reverse life-threatening airway swelling and blood pressure drop, buying critical time until emergency medical care arrives.

What is the difference between epinephrine and antihistamines in allergic reactions?
Epinephrine treats life-threatening anaphylaxis rapidly, while antihistamines treat mild to moderate allergic symptoms more slowly.

What is the primary purpose of heat-related illness first aid?
To recognize heat exhaustion vs heat stroke, provide cooling and hydration, and prevent progression to life-threatening conditions.

What is the difference between heat exhaustion and heat stroke?
Heat exhaustion causes heavy sweating and weakness treatable with cooling, while heat stroke causes hot dry skin and requires emergency cooling.

What is the primary purpose of cold-related illness management?
To recognize hypothermia vs frostbite, provide gradual warming, and prevent tissue damage and life-threatening complications.

What is the difference between hypothermia and frostbite?
Hypothermia involves whole-body temperature drop affecting vital functions, while frostbite involves localized tissue freezing in extremities.

What is the primary purpose of poisoning first aid?
To identify poison type, prevent further absorption, and provide appropriate treatment while contacting poison control for specific guidance.

What is the difference between corrosive and non-corrosive poisoning?
Corrosive poisons cause chemical burns requiring immediate dilution, while non-corrosive poisons may require induced vomiting or activated charcoal.

What is the primary purpose of poison control centers?
To provide expert guidance for poisoning emergencies, offering specific treatment recommendations based on poison type, amount, and patient condition.

What is the difference between local poison control and emergency services?
Poison control provides specific treatment guidance, while emergency services provide immediate life support and transport when needed.

What is the primary purpose of psychological first aid?
To provide emotional support and coping assistance during crises, reducing stress and promoting resilience in traumatic situations.

What is the difference between psychological and physical first aid?
Psychological first aid addresses emotional and mental health needs, while physical first aid addresses bodily injuries and medical conditions.

What is the primary purpose of documentation in first aid?
To record treatment provided, patient condition changes, and communication with emergency services for continuity of care.

What is the difference between treatment notes and incident reports?
Treatment notes document care provided, while incident reports describe the event and circumstances for legal and administrative purposes.

What is the primary purpose of first aid kit maintenance?
To ensure supplies are available, sterile, and within expiration dates for effective emergency response when needed.

What is the difference between personal and workplace first aid kits?
Personal kits contain basic supplies for individual use, while workplace kits contain comprehensive supplies for multiple people and various injuries.

What is the primary purpose of first aid training and certification?
To develop knowledge, skills, and confidence for effective emergency response, maintaining current skills through regular practice and updates.

What is the difference between basic and advanced first aid certification?
Basic certification covers common emergencies and life support, while advanced certification includes specialized skills and medical assessment techniques.

What is the primary purpose of continuing education in first aid?
To maintain current knowledge of guidelines, practice skills regularly, and learn new techniques for improved emergency response effectiveness.

What is the difference between certification and recertification in first aid?
Initial certification provides first-time training, while recertification updates skills and knowledge to maintain current competency.
