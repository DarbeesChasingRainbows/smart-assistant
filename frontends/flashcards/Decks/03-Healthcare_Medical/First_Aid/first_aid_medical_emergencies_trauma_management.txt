What is the primary purpose of triage in multiple casualty incidents?
To prioritize patients based on injury severity and treatment urgency, ensuring limited resources help those most likely to survive.

What is the difference between immediate and delayed triage categories?
Immediate (red) patients need life-saving treatment now, while delayed (yellow) patients have serious injuries but can wait for treatment.

What is the primary purpose of shock recognition and management?
To identify inadequate tissue perfusion and provide rapid intervention to prevent organ failure and death through positioning, oxygen, and fluid management.

What is the difference between hypovolemic and cardiogenic shock?
Hypovolemic shock results from fluid loss requiring fluid replacement, while cardiogenic shock results from heart pump failure requiring cardiac support.

What is the primary purpose of airway management in trauma patients?
To ensure patent airway for adequate oxygenation, using positioning, suction, and advanced airway techniques to prevent respiratory failure.

What is the difference between basic and advanced airway management?
Basic airway uses head-tilt chin-lift and OPA/NPA, while advanced airway includes endotracheal intubation and surgical airways for professional providers.

What is the primary purpose of cervical spine immobilization in trauma?
To prevent spinal cord damage from unstable neck injuries, using manual stabilization and cervical collars until spinal injury is ruled out.

What is the difference between manual and mechanical cervical immobilization?
Manual immobilization uses hands to hold head position temporarily, while mechanical uses collars and blocks for prolonged immobilization.

What is the primary purpose of chest trauma assessment in first aid?
To identify life-threatening conditions like tension pneumothorax, hemothorax, and cardiac tamponade requiring immediate decompression or treatment.

What is the difference between open and closed chest injuries?
Open chest injuries have chest wall wounds requiring sealing, while closed injuries have internal damage potentially needing decompression.

What is the primary purpose of abdominal trauma management?
To recognize internal bleeding and organ injury, providing rapid transport and avoiding oral intake to prevent complications during surgery.

What is the difference between blunt and penetrating abdominal trauma?
Blunt trauma causes internal injury from force without skin break, while penetrating trauma has object entry causing direct organ damage.

What is the primary purpose of pelvic fracture recognition in trauma?
To identify potentially life-threatening internal bleeding requiring stabilization and rapid transport, as pelvic fractures can cause massive blood loss.

What is the difference between stable and unstable pelvic fractures?
Stable fractures have minimal bleeding risk, while unstable fractures can disrupt blood vessels causing life-threatening hemorrhage.

What is the primary purpose of extremity trauma assessment in first aid?
To identify fractures, dislocations, and vascular injuries requiring immobilization and potential urgent intervention to prevent limb loss.

What is the difference between open and closed fractures?
Open fractures have skin break with infection risk requiring urgent care, while closed fractures have intact skin with lower immediate risk.

What is the primary purpose of neurovascular assessment in limb injuries?
To check circulation, sensation, and movement before and after splinting to detect compartment syndrome or vascular compromise requiring urgent care.

What is the difference between compartment syndrome and normal swelling?
Compartment syndrome causes severe pain, numbness, and pulse changes requiring emergency surgery, while normal swelling causes mild discomfort.

What is the primary purpose of burn severity assessment in trauma?
To determine depth, size, and location to establish treatment priority and need for specialized burn center care versus local treatment.

What is the difference between partial and full thickness burns?
Partial thickness burns affect skin layers with pain and blistering, while full thickness burns destroy all skin layers with painless white appearance.

What is the primary purpose of inhalation injury recognition in burn patients?
To identify airway damage from smoke/heat requiring intubation and specialized care, as airway swelling can progress rapidly to obstruction.

What is the difference between upper and lower airway burns?
Upper airway burns cause facial burns and hoarseness requiring observation, while lower airway burns cause respiratory distress requiring intubation.

What is the primary purpose of electrical injury assessment in first aid?
To identify entry/exit wounds, cardiac arrhythmias, and internal tissue damage requiring cardiac monitoring and specialized medical evaluation.

What is the difference between high and low voltage electrical injuries?
High voltage causes extensive internal damage and cardiac effects, while low voltage typically causes local burn injuries at contact points.

What is the primary purpose of chemical burn management in first aid?
To remove chemical agent, neutralize if appropriate, and prevent continued tissue damage while identifying specific chemical for proper treatment.

What is the difference between acid and alkali chemical burns?
Acids cause coagulation necrosis limiting penetration, while alkalis cause liquefaction necrosis allowing deeper tissue penetration.

What is the primary purpose of radiation exposure management in first aid?
To limit continued exposure, decontaminate patients, and prevent secondary contamination while assessing dose for medical treatment priorities.

What is the difference between external and internal radiation contamination?
External contamination has radioactive material on body surface requiring decontamination, while internal contamination requires medical decorporation treatment.

What is the primary purpose of crush syndrome recognition in trauma?
To identify muscle breakdown products causing kidney failure and cardiac arrhythmias requiring IV fluids and monitoring before extrication.

What is the difference between crush injury and compartment syndrome?
Crush injury involves prolonged compression affecting entire limb, while compartment syndrome involves swelling within muscle compartments.

What is the primary purpose of drowning rescue and resuscitation?
To provide rapid rescue, start CPR if needed, and prevent secondary drowning complications through oxygen therapy and medical observation.

What is the difference between wet and dry drowning?
Wet drowning involves water in lungs requiring respiratory support, while dry drowning involves laryngospasm without water aspiration.

What is the primary purpose of near-drowning management?
To provide oxygen therapy, monitor for respiratory deterioration, and prevent secondary complications like pneumonia and ARDS.

What is the difference between saltwater and freshwater drowning effects?
Saltwater causes pulmonary edema through fluid shift, while freshwater causes surfactant destruction leading to alveolar collapse.

What is the primary purpose of altitude sickness recognition and management?
To identify symptoms of acute mountain sickness and prevent progression to life-threatening high altitude cerebral or pulmonary edema.

What is the difference between acute mountain sickness and high altitude cerebral edema?
Acute mountain sickness causes headache and nausea treatable with descent, while HACE causes confusion and ataxia requiring immediate descent.

What is the primary purpose of decompression sickness management in diving emergencies?
To provide emergency oxygen and rapid hyperbaric chamber treatment for bubble formation in tissues and bloodstream.

What is the difference between Type I and Type II decompression sickness?
Type I causes joint pain and skin manifestations, while Type II causes neurological symptoms requiring urgent treatment.

What is the primary purpose of barotrauma management in diving emergencies?
To treat pressure-related injuries to lungs, ears, and sinuses, preventing complications like pneumothorax and hearing loss.

What is the difference between pulmonary and sinus barotrauma?
Pulmonary barotrauma can cause lung collapse and air embolism, while sinus barotrauma causes facial pain and sinus bleeding.

What is the primary purpose of environmental hyperthermia management?
To rapidly cool body while monitoring for complications like heat stroke causing organ damage and potential death.

What is the difference between heat exhaustion and heat stroke treatment?
Heat exhaustion requires rehydration and rest, while heat stroke requires rapid cooling and emergency medical care for potential organ failure.

What is the primary purpose of environmental hypothermia management?
To prevent further heat loss, provide gradual rewarming, and monitor for cardiac arrhythmias during rewarming process.

What is the difference between mild and severe hypothermia treatment?
Mild hypothermia uses passive warming with blankets, while severe hypothermia requires active internal warming and cardiac monitoring.

What is the primary purpose of frostbite management in cold injuries?
To prevent further tissue freezing, provide controlled rewarming, and protect injured tissue from mechanical damage and infection.

What is the difference between superficial and deep frostbite?
Superficial frostbite affects skin layers with full recovery, while deep frostbite affects deeper tissues potentially requiring amputation.

What is the primary purpose of lightning strike injury management?
To assess for cardiac arrest, neurological injury, and burns while providing immediate resuscitation and rapid transport to specialized care.

What is the difference between direct and indirect lightning injuries?
Direct strikes cause severe burns and cardiac effects, while indirect effects cause neurological and blast injuries from current spread.

What is the primary purpose of snakebite identification and management?
To identify venomous vs non-venomous bites, provide appropriate first aid, and rapidly transport for antivenom administration if needed.

What is the difference between neurotoxic and hemotoxic snake venom?
Neurotoxic venom causes paralysis and respiratory failure, while hemotoxic venom causes bleeding and tissue destruction.

What is the primary purpose of insect sting allergy management?
To recognize anaphylaxis, administer epinephrine immediately, and provide airway support while seeking emergency medical care.

What is the difference between local allergic reactions and anaphylaxis?
Local reactions cause swelling and pain at sting site, while anaphylaxis causes systemic effects requiring epinephrine.

What is the primary purpose of marine life injury management?
To identify specific injuries (jellyfish, stingrays, stonefish) and provide appropriate first aid including venom neutralization and pain control.

What is the difference between jellyfish and stingray injuries?
Jellyfish stings require vinegar neutralization, while stingray injuries require hot water immersion for pain relief.

What is the primary purpose of diabetic emergency recognition in first aid?
To differentiate hypoglycemia from hyperglycemia and provide appropriate treatment with sugar or insulin while preventing complications.

What is the difference between diabetic ketoacidosis and hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state?
DKA occurs in type 1 diabetes with ketones, while HHS occurs in type 2 diabetes with extreme hyperglycemia without ketones.

What is the primary purpose of seizure emergency management?
To protect from injury, maintain airway, time the seizure, and provide post-seizure care while identifying seizure type for treatment.

What is the difference between generalized and focal seizures?
Generalized seizures affect both brain hemispheres with loss of consciousness, while focal seizures affect one area with varied symptoms.

What is the primary purpose of stroke recognition and management?
To identify stroke symptoms using FAST (Face, Arms, Speech, Time) and provide rapid transport for time-sensitive treatment.

What is the difference between ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke?
Ischemic stroke involves blood clots treatable with clot-busting drugs, while hemorrhagic stroke involves bleeding requiring different treatment.

What is the primary purpose of heart attack recognition in first aid?
To identify chest pain symptoms, call emergency services immediately, and provide aspirin and comfort while preventing complications.

What is the difference between angina and heart attack symptoms?
Angina causes brief chest pain relieved by rest, while heart attack causes persistent pain with other symptoms requiring emergency care.

What is the primary purpose of asthma emergency management?
To assist with rescue inhaler use, provide calm environment, and recognize severe attacks requiring emergency medical intervention.

What is the difference between mild and severe asthma attacks?
Mild attacks improve with rescue inhaler, while severe attacks cause severe distress and require emergency medical treatment.

What is the primary purpose of COPD emergency management?
To provide oxygen, assist with breathing medications, and recognize exacerbations requiring antibiotics or hospitalization.

What is the difference between asthma and COPD emergencies?
Asthma attacks typically reverse completely, while COPD exacerbations may have incomplete recovery and require different management.

What is the primary purpose of anaphylaxis recognition and treatment?
To identify severe allergic reaction affecting airway and breathing, administer epinephrine immediately, and provide airway support.

What is the difference between mild allergic reaction and anaphylaxis?
Mild reactions cause localized symptoms, while anaphylaxis causes systemic effects requiring epinephrine and emergency care.

What is the primary purpose of psychiatric emergency management in first aid?
To ensure safety of patient and others, provide de-escalation techniques, and arrange appropriate mental health evaluation.

What is the difference between anxiety attack and psychotic emergency?
Anxiety attacks require calm reassurance, while psychotic emergencies may require medication and psychiatric evaluation.

What is the primary purpose of substance overdose recognition and management?
To identify substance type, provide airway protection, and administer specific antidotes when available while preventing complications.

What is the difference between opioid and stimulant overdose?
Opioid overdose causes respiratory depression treatable with naloxone, while stimulant overdose causes agitation and cardiac effects.

What is the primary purpose of pediatric emergency assessment differences?
To recognize that children present differently than adults, have unique injury patterns, and require age-appropriate treatment approaches.

What is the difference between adult and pediatric airway anatomy?
Children have larger tongues and narrower airways making obstruction more likely, requiring different positioning and airway techniques.

What is the primary purpose of geriatric emergency considerations?
To recognize atypical presentations, multiple medications, and comorbidities affecting emergency assessment and treatment in elderly patients.

What is the difference between elderly and adult emergency presentations?
Elderly patients often have subtle symptoms and multiple chronic conditions requiring different assessment priorities.

What is the primary purpose of pregnancy-related emergency management?
To protect both mother and fetus, recognize pregnancy-specific conditions, and provide appropriate positioning and care.

What is the difference between normal and emergency pregnancy symptoms?
Normal pregnancy has common discomforts, while emergencies involve severe pain, bleeding, or blood pressure changes requiring urgent care.

What is the primary purpose of disaster medicine principles in first aid?
To apply triage, resource allocation, and mass casualty management when normal medical systems are overwhelmed.

What is the difference between routine and disaster first aid?
Routine first aid focuses on individual patients, while disaster first aid prioritizes population survival with limited resources.
