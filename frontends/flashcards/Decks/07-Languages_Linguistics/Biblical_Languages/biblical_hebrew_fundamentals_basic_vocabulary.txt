What is the primary purpose of studying Biblical Hebrew today?
To read the Old Testament/Hebrew Bible in its original language, understanding the precise meaning, poetic beauty, and theological depth of the Hebrew Scriptures.

What is the basic Hebrew alphabet and pronunciation system?
22 consonants: א (aleph), ב (bet), ג (gimel), ד (dalet), ה (he), ו (vav), ז (zayin), ח (chet), ט (tet), י (yod), כ (kaf), ל (lamed), מ (mem), נ (nun), ס (samekh), ע (ayin), פ (pe), צ (tsadi), ק (qof), ר (resh), ש (sin/shin), ת (tav), with vowel points for pronunciation.

What is the difference between consonantal text and pointed text in Biblical Hebrew?
Consonantal text (unpointed) contains only consonants as in ancient manuscripts, while pointed text includes vowel points and accent marks added by Masoretes for pronunciation.

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew vowel points (niqqud) in Biblical study?
To indicate vowel sounds and pronunciation, preserving the traditional reading of the Hebrew text after consonantal writing became standard.

What are the basic Hebrew vowel categories and their functions?
Long vowels (qamets, patah, tsere), short vowels (sheva, hireq, seghol), reduced vowels (hateph qamets, hateph patah, hateph seghol), and shewa (silent or vocal).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew accents (ta'amim) in Biblical study?
To indicate cantillation for liturgical reading and show syntactical relationships between words and phrases in the text.

What is the difference between disjunctive and conjunctive accents in Hebrew?
Disjunctive accents mark major syntactical divisions (atnah, sof pasuq), while conjunctive accents connect words within phrases (merkha, tiphcha).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew syllables in pronunciation and grammar?
To organize consonants and vowels into pronounceable units, determining stress patterns and affecting grammatical forms.

What is the difference between open and closed syllables in Hebrew?
Open syllables end with a vowel (בָּנָי = banay), while closed syllables end with a consonant (בֵּן = ben), affecting vowel length and stress.

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew stress patterns in the language?
To indicate which syllable receives emphasis, typically on the last syllable (milra) or penultimate syllable (mil'el), affecting meaning and grammar.

What is the difference between milra and mil'el stress in Hebrew words?
Milra stress emphasizes the final syllable (most common), while mil'el stress emphasizes the penultimate syllable, often in segolate nouns and certain forms.

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew noun states in grammar?
To show relationships between nouns, with absolute state standing alone and construct state showing possession or connection to following noun.

What is the difference between absolute and construct state in Hebrew nouns?
Absolute state stands independently (מֶלֶךְ = king), while construct state connects to following noun (מֶלֶךְ יִשְׂרָאֵל = king of Israel).

What is the basic pattern of Hebrew singular masculine nouns?
Typically consist of root consonants with vowel patterns, often ending in consonants (אִישׁ = man, סֵפֶר = book, יוֹם = day).

What is the difference between masculine and feminine singular nouns in Hebrew?
Masculine nouns typically have no special ending (אִישׁ), while feminine nouns often end in הָ- (אִשָּׁה) or ת- (יָלְדָה).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew plural formations in grammar?
To indicate number (more than one) through specific patterns, with masculine plural typically ending in ים- and feminine plural ending in וֹת-.

What is the difference between masculine and feminine plural patterns in Hebrew?
Masculine plural ends in ים- (אִישִׁים = men), while feminine plural ends in וֹת- (אִשָּׁוֹת = women), with some variations.

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew dual formations in grammar?
To indicate pairs of things (body parts, natural pairs) using the יַם- ending (יָדַיִם = hands, עֵינַיִם = eyes).

What is the difference between dual and plural forms in Hebrew?
Dual specifically indicates pairs (eyes, hands, ears), while plural indicates any number more than one, with dual being a special subset of plural.

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew definite articles in syntax?
To specify definiteness, prefixed as הַ- to nouns and changing vowel patterns (הַסֵּפֶר = the book, הָאִישׁ = the man).

What is the difference between Hebrew and English definite articles?
Hebrew prefixes הַ- to the beginning of nouns with vowel changes, while English uses separate words before nouns.

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for common objects and concepts?
סֵפֶר (book), שֻׁלְחָן (table), כִּסֵּא (chair), בַּיִת (house), דֶּלֶת (door), חַלֹּנִית (window), אֹהֶל (tent), מִטָּה (bed).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew prepositions in grammar?
To show relationships between words, often as separate words (אֶל, עִם) or prefixed as inseparable prepositions (בְּ-, כְּ-, לְּ-).

What is the difference between separate and inseparable prepositions in Hebrew?
Separate prepositions stand as individual words (אֶל = to), while inseparable prepositions are prefixed to nouns (בְּבַּיִת = in the house).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for family relationships?
אָב (father), אֵם (mother), בֵּן (son), בַּת (daughter), אָח (brother), אָחוֹת (sister), אִישׁ (man/husband), אִשָּׁה (woman/wife).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew personal pronouns in grammar?
To indicate person and number (I, you, he, she, we, you, they), with separate forms for independent and suffix pronouns.

What is the difference between independent and suffix pronouns in Hebrew?
Independent pronouns stand alone (אֲנִי = I), while suffix pronouns attach to nouns, verbs, or prepositions (סִפְרִי = my book).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for numbers and counting?
אֶחָד (one), שְׁנַיִם (two), שְׁלוֹשָׁה (three), אַרְבַּעָה (four), חֲמִשָּׁה (five), עֶשֶׂר (ten), מֵאָה (hundred), אֶלֶף (thousand).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew verb roots (shorashim) in the language?
To provide the basic three-consonant core meaning that appears throughout all verb forms and related nouns.

What is the difference between strong and weak verbs in Hebrew?
Strong verbs maintain all three root consonants throughout all forms (כָּתַב), while weak verbs lose or change one or more consonants in certain forms (אָמַר).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for common actions?
הָלַךְ (he went), בָּא (he came), אָמַר (he said), שָׁמַע (he heard), רָאָה (he saw), אָכַל (he ate), שָׁתָה (he drank), יָשַׁב (he sat).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew verb stems (binyanim) in grammar?
To create different meanings and grammatical relationships from the same root through systematic patterns.

What is the basic pattern of the Qal stem in Hebrew verbs?
The simple, active stem representing basic action (כָּתַב = he wrote), serving as the foundation for other stems.

What is the difference between Qal and Niphal stems in Hebrew?
Qal is simple active (he wrote), while Niphal is simple passive/reflexive (he was written/he wrote himself).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew perfect tense in verbs?
To indicate completed actions, typically translated as past tense in English, with 3ms ending -ָתִי (כָּתַבְתִּי = I wrote).

What is the difference between perfect and imperfect tenses in Hebrew?
Perfect tense shows completed action (כָּתַב = he wrote), while imperfect shows incomplete or future action (יִכְתֹּב = he will write/he writes).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for religious and spiritual concepts?
אֱלֹהִים (God), יְהוָה (YHWH/LORD), אֲדֹנָי (Lord), רוּחַ (spirit), נֶפֶשׁ (soul/life), חֵן (grace), חֶסֶד (lovingkindness), אֱמֶת (truth), שָׁלוֹם (peace).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew imperfect tense in verbs?
To indicate incomplete, ongoing, or future actions, with 3ms ending (יִכְתֹּב = he will write/he writes).

What is the difference between imperfect consecutive and imperfect simple in Hebrew?
Imperfect consecutive with vav conversive converts to past tense (וַיִּכְתֹּב = and he wrote), while imperfect simple maintains future/incomplete meaning.

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for places and locations?
שָׁמַיִם (heaven), אָרֶץ (earth), יְרוּשָׁלַ‍ִם (Jerusalem), בֵּית־לֶחֶם (Bethlehem), מִצְרַיִם (Egypt), בָּבֶל (Babylon), הַר (mountain), נָהָר (river).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew imperative mood in verbs?
To express commands, with 2ms form typically identical to imperfect without prefix (כְּתֹב = write!).

What is the difference between masculine and feminine imperative forms in Hebrew?
Masculine imperative uses base form (כְּתֹב = write!), while feminine adds הִי- ending (כִּתְבִי = write!).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for time expressions?
הַיּוֹם (today), מָחָר (tomorrow), אֶתְמוֹל (yesterday), עַתָּה (now), אָז (then), בֹּקֶר (morning), עֶרֶב (evening), לַיְלָה (night).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew infinitive constructs in grammar?
To function as verbal nouns, often expressing purpose or result, typically with prefix לְ־ (לִכְתֹּב = to write).

What is the difference between infinitive construct and infinitive absolute in Hebrew?
Infinitive construct functions as verbal noun with prefixes (לִכְתֹּב = to write), while infinitive absolute emphasizes the action itself (כָּתוֹב כָּתוֹב = surely writing).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for animals and creatures?
שׁוֹר (ox), חֲמוֹר (donkey), שֶׂה (sheep), עֵז (goat), כֶּלֶב (dog), אַרְיֵה (lion), דָּג (fish), עוֹף (bird), תַּנִּינָא (dragon).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew participles in grammar?
To function as verbal adjectives, describing ongoing action or state (כֹּתֵב = writing), with both active and passive forms.

What is the difference between active and passive participles in Hebrew?
Active participles describe ongoing action (כֹּתֵב = writing), while passive participles describe being acted upon (כָּתוּב = having been written).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for food and drink?
לֶחֶם (bread), יַיִן (wine), מַיִם (water), בָּשָׂר (meat), דָּגָה (fish), חָלָב (milk), דְּבַשׁ (honey), שֶׁמֶן (oil), מֶלַח (salt).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew demonstrative pronouns?
To point out specific people or things (זֶה = this, הַהוּא = that), with gender and number distinctions.

What is the difference between זֶה and זֹאת in Hebrew demonstratives?
זֶה is masculine singular (this), while זֹאת is feminine singular (this), with corresponding plural forms (אֵלֶּה = these).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for colors and qualities?
לָבָן (white), שָׁחֹר (black), אָדֹם (red), יָרֹק (green), גָּדוֹל (big), קָטָן (small), טוֹב (good), רַע (bad), יָפֶה (beautiful).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew interrogative pronouns and adverbs?
To form questions about identity (מִי = who), quantity (כַּמָּה = how much), time (מָתַי = when), place (אֵיפֹה = where).

What is the difference between מִי and מַה in Hebrew questions?
מִי asks about people (who?), while מַה asks about things (what?), both with various forms for different contexts.

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for natural world?
שֶׁמֶשׁ (sun), יָרֵחַ (moon), כּוֹכָב (star), אוֹר (light), חֹשֶׁךְ (darkness), אֵשׁ (fire), מַיִם (water), רוּחַ (wind), אֲדָמָה (ground).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew negative particles in grammar?
To negate statements, commands, and questions, primarily using לֹא for indicative statements and אַל for imperative commands.

What is the difference between לֹא and אַל in Hebrew negation?
לֹא negates indicative statements and questions (לֹא הָלַךְ = he did not go), while אַל negates imperative commands (אַל תֵּלֵךְ = do not go).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for body parts?
רֹאשׁ (head), עַיִן (eye), אֹזֶן (ear), פֶּה (mouth), יָד (hand), רֶגֶל (foot), לֵבָב (heart), נֶפֶשׁ (soul/life), גּוּף (body).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew conjunctions in grammar?
To connect words, phrases, and clauses, showing relationships like addition (וְ = and), contrast (אֶלָּא = but), condition (אִם = if).

What is the difference between וְ as connective and וְ as consecutive in Hebrew?
Connective וְ joins elements (אִישׁ וְאִשָּׁה = man and woman), while consecutive וְ converts imperfect to past tense (וַיֹּאמֶר = and he said).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for clothing and personal items?
בֶּגֶד (garment), מַחֲזַיִים (sandals), אֵזוֹר (belt), כְּתֹנֶת (tunic), מְעִיל (cloak), צִיצִת (tassel), נֵזֶם (ring), נֵר (lamp).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew relative clauses in complex sentences?
To modify nouns with additional information, typically introduced by אֲשֶׁר (who/which/that) or asher.

What is the difference between אֲשֶׁר and שֶׁ in Hebrew relative clauses?
אֲשֶׁר is the standard relative pronoun (the man who came), while שֶׁ is a shorter form used in later Hebrew and poetry.

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for emotions and states?
שִׂמְחָה (joy), עֶצֶב (sorrow), יִרְאָה (fear), אַהֲבָה (love), שָׁלוֹם (peace), כָּעַס (anger), חֶסֶד (kindness), אֱמוּנָה (faith).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew adjectives in grammar?
To describe nouns, agreeing in gender, number, and definiteness, typically following the noun they modify.

What is the difference between attributive and predicate adjectives in Hebrew?
Attributive adjectives follow nouns (אִישׁ טוֹב = good man), while predicate adjectives follow copula implied (הָאִישׁ טוֹב = the man is good).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for social relationships and status?
רֵעַ (friend), אוֹיֵב (enemy), עֶבֶד (servant), אָדוֹן (lord/master), מֶלֶךְ (king), כֹּהֵן (priest), נָבִיא (prophet), זָקֵן (elder), יֶלֶד (child).

What is the primary purpose of Hebrew numerals beyond basic counting?
To express ordinal numbers (first, second), fractions, and large numbers using specific constructions and patterns.

What is the difference between cardinal and ordinal numbers in Hebrew?
Cardinal numbers indicate quantity (אֶחָד = one), while ordinal numbers indicate position (רִאשׁוֹן = first).

What is the basic Hebrew vocabulary for legal and administrative terms?
תּוֹרָה (law), מִשְׁפָּט (judgment), חֹק (statute), מִצְוָה (commandment), עֵד (witness), בְּרִית (covenant), סֵפֶר (document/book), חֹתָם (seal).
