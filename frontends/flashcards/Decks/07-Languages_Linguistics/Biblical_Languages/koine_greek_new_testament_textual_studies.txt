What is the primary purpose of studying the Greek New Testament?
To read the Christian Scriptures in their original language, understanding the precise meaning, theological nuances, and literary artistry of the biblical texts.

What is the difference between Koine Greek and Classical Greek in the New Testament?
Koine Greek developed simplified grammar, expanded vocabulary, and different pronunciation from Classical Greek, becoming the common language of the Hellenistic world.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament textual criticism?
To reconstruct the original text of the New Testament by comparing manuscript variants and identifying copying errors and intentional changes.

What is the difference between Alexandrian and Byzantine text types in New Testament manuscripts?
Alexandrian texts are generally older and more concise (Codex Vaticanus, Sinaiticus), while Byzantine texts are later and more numerous (Textus Receptus tradition).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament manuscript families?
To group related manuscripts that share common readings and errors, helping trace the history of text transmission and identify original readings.

What is the difference between papyri, uncials, and minuscules in New Testament manuscripts?
Papyri are ancient fragments on papyrus (P52, P46), uncials are capital letter manuscripts (Codex Vaticanus), while minuscules use later cursive script.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament textual variants?
To show differences between manuscript readings that require evaluation for determining the most likely original text.

What is the difference between intentional and unintentional variants in New Testament manuscripts?
Unintentional variants result from copying errors (homoioteleuton, itacism), while intentional variants reflect theological or stylistic changes by scribes.

What is the primary purpose of the Nestle-Aland Novum Testamentum Graece?
To provide the standard critical edition of the Greek New Testament with extensive apparatus showing manuscript variants and textual evidence.

What is the difference between the critical text and Textus Receptus in New Testament editions?
Critical text (Nestle-Aland) uses modern textual criticism with manuscript evidence, while Textus Receptus follows Byzantine tradition used for early translations.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament genre analysis?
To understand different literary types (gospel, epistle, apocalypse, history) and their characteristic features for proper interpretation.

What is the difference between gospel genre and epistolary genre in the New Testament?
Gospels present narrative accounts of Jesus' life and teachings, while epistles are letters addressing specific communities and theological issues.

What is the primary purpose of synoptic gospel analysis?
To understand the literary relationships between Matthew, Mark, and Luke through comparing their similarities and differences.

What is the difference between the Two-Source Hypothesis and Four-Source Hypothesis in synoptic studies?
Two-Source Hypothesis proposes Mark and Q as sources for Matthew and Luke, while Four-Source Hypothesis adds M and L materials unique to Matthew and Luke.

What is the primary purpose of Johannine literature analysis?
To understand the unique theology, literary style, and community context of John's Gospel, epistles, and Revelation.

What is the difference between Johannine theology and synoptic theology in the New Testament?
Johannine theology emphasizes Christ's divine nature, love, and eternal life, while synoptic theology focuses more on kingdom of God and ethical teachings.

What is the primary purpose of Pauline epistle analysis?
To understand Paul's theological arguments, pastoral concerns, and rhetorical strategies in addressing different early Christian communities.

What is the difference between Paul's major epistles and pastoral epistles in theology and style?
Major epistles (Romans, Corinthians) develop systematic theology, while pastoral epistles (Timothy, Titus) focus on church leadership and practical concerns.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament Christology studies?
To analyze how different New Testament writers understand and present Jesus Christ's nature, role, and significance.

What is the difference between high Christology and low Christology in the New Testament?
High Christology emphasizes Jesus' divine nature and pre-existence (John 1, Colossians 1), while low Christology focuses on his humanity and messianic role (Synoptics).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament eschatology studies?
To understand teachings about last things, including death, judgment, heaven, and the ultimate fulfillment of God's purposes.

What is the difference between realized eschatology and futurist eschatology in the New Testament?
Realized eschatology sees kingdom blessings as present (Luke 17:21), while futurist eschatology expects future fulfillment (Revelation, 2 Thessalonians).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament ecclesiology studies?
To understand the nature, purpose, and organization of the church as presented in various New Testament writings.

What is the difference between Pauline ecclesiology and Matthean ecclesiology?
Pauline ecclesiology emphasizes the church as Christ's body with diverse gifts (1 Corinthians 12), while Matthean ecclesiology focuses on church authority and discipline (Matthew 16, 18).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament soteriology studies?
To analyze different understandings of salvation, including justification, sanctification, and glorification across New Testament writings.

What is the difference between Pauline and Johannine concepts of salvation in the New Testament?
Paul emphasizes justification by faith and union with Christ (Romans, Galatians), while John focuses on eternal life and abiding in Christ (John, 1 John).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament hermeneutics?
To develop principles and methods for interpreting New Testament texts accurately in their original historical and literary contexts.

What is the difference between historical-critical and literary approaches to New Testament interpretation?
Historical-critical methods focus on original context and authorial intent, while literary approaches emphasize narrative structure and reader response.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament exegesis?
To draw out the meaning of biblical texts through careful analysis of grammar, syntax, historical context, and literary features.

What is the difference between exegesis and eisegesis in New Testament study?
Exegesis draws meaning out of the text through careful analysis, while eisegesis reads meaning into the text based on presuppositions.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament theology?
To understand the major theological themes and concepts that emerge from the New Testament as a whole and in individual books.

What is the difference between biblical theology and systematic theology in New Testament studies?
Biblical theology traces themes within their historical context, while systematic theology organizes doctrines into coherent systems using philosophical categories.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament background studies?
To understand the historical, cultural, religious, and literary contexts that shaped the writing and meaning of New Testament texts.

What is the difference between Jewish and Greco-Roman backgrounds for New Testament study?
Jewish background provides Hebrew Scriptures, temple worship, and messianic expectations, while Greco-Roman background offers Hellenistic language, philosophy, and social structures.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament archaeology?
To uncover physical evidence that illuminates the historical and cultural context of New Testament events and teachings.

What is the difference between archaeological evidence for New Testament sites versus customs?
Site archaeology locates specific places mentioned in the New Testament, while cultural archaeology reveals daily life, architecture, and material culture.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament patristics?
To study how early church fathers interpreted and used New Testament texts, understanding the development of Christian doctrine and exegesis.

What is the difference between Alexandrian and Antiochian patristic exegesis?
Alexandrian exegesis uses allegorical and spiritual interpretation (Origen), while Antiochian exegesis emphasizes historical and literal meaning (Chrysostom, Theodore).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament canon studies?
To understand how the 27 books were selected and recognized as authoritative Scripture, and the criteria for canonicity.

What is the difference between canonical and non-canonical New Testament writings?
Canonical writings were recognized as inspired Scripture by the early church, while non-canonical writings (apocrypha, pseudepigrapha) were not included in the canon.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament translation studies?
To understand principles and challenges of translating Greek texts into different languages while preserving meaning and style.

What is the difference between formal equivalence and dynamic equivalence in New Testament translation?
Formal equivalence emphasizes word-for-word translation (NASB, ESV), while dynamic equivalence focuses on thought-for-thought meaning (NLT, CEV).

What is the primary purpose of New Testament discourse analysis?
To study how texts communicate at levels beyond the sentence, including cohesion, rhetoric, and thematic development.

What is the difference between micro-level and macro-level discourse analysis in New Testament study?
Micro-level analysis examines sentence connections and cohesion, while macro-level studies overall structure and argument flow.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament socio-rhetorical criticism?
To understand how texts function as rhetoric in specific social contexts, examining power dynamics, cultural values, and persuasive strategies.

What is the difference between rhetorical criticism and social-scientific criticism in New Testament study?
Rhetorical criticism analyzes persuasive techniques and argument structure, while social-scientific criticism examines cultural patterns and social institutions.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament narrative criticism?
To understand how biblical stories function as narratives with characters, plot, setting, and thematic development.

What is the difference between narrative criticism and historical criticism in New Testament study?
Narrative criticism focuses on story elements and literary artistry, while historical criticism investigates the historical events behind the narratives.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament reader-response criticism?
To examine how readers interact with and interpret texts, recognizing that meaning emerges in the reading process.

What is the difference between authorial intent and reader response in New Testament interpretation?
Authorial intent focuses on what the original writer meant to communicate, while reader response considers how modern readers understand and apply texts.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament postcolonial criticism?
To examine biblical texts in light of imperial power dynamics, cultural domination, and resistance movements.

What is the difference between imperial and postcolonial readings of the New Testament?
Imperial readings examine how texts interact with Roman power, while postcolonial readings focus on resistance to domination and alternative visions.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament feminist criticism?
To analyze texts regarding gender roles, women's experiences, and patriarchal structures in biblical texts and their interpretation.

What is the difference between feminist and womanist approaches to New Testament interpretation?
Feminist criticism generally focuses on gender from women's perspectives, while womanist criticism specifically addresses the experiences of women of color.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament ecological criticism?
To examine biblical perspectives on creation, environment, and human relationship with the natural world.

What is the difference between ecological and creation theology readings of the New Testament?
Ecological criticism focuses on environmental ethics and creation care, while creation theology emphasizes God's relationship to all creation.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament intertextuality studies?
To understand how New Testament writers quote, allude to, and transform Old Testament and other contemporary texts.

What is the difference between quotation and allusion in New Testament intertextuality?
Quotation uses explicit citation formulas ("it is written"), while allusion makes indirect references without explicit attribution.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament reception history?
To trace how biblical texts have been interpreted and used throughout church history and in various cultural contexts.

What is the difference between historical reception and contemporary appropriation of New Testament texts?
Historical reception studies past interpretations and uses, while contemporary appropriation examines current applications and meanings.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament performance criticism?
To understand how biblical texts were originally performed orally and how performance affects interpretation and meaning.

What is the difference between oral and written performance in New Testament contexts?
Oral performance emphasizes memory, rhythm, and audience interaction, while written performance focuses on public reading and liturgical use.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament linguistic analysis?
To study how language features contribute to meaning, style, and rhetorical effect in biblical texts.

What is the difference between phonological and syntactic analysis in New Testament linguistics?
Phonological analysis studies sound patterns and pronunciation, while syntactic analysis examines sentence structure and grammatical relationships.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament semantic analysis?
To understand word meanings, conceptual relationships, and how vocabulary shapes theological expression.

What is the difference between lexical and conceptual semantics in New Testament study?
Lexical semantics studies individual word meanings, while conceptual semantics examines how words form conceptual networks and theological frameworks.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament pragmatic analysis?
To understand how language functions in communication contexts, including speech acts, presuppositions, and implicature.

What is the difference between semantic and pragmatic meaning in New Testament texts?
Semantic meaning deals with literal word and sentence meanings, while pragmatic meaning considers context, speaker intent, and communicative effect.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament cognitive linguistics?
To understand how conceptual structures, metaphors, and mental models shape theological expression and understanding.

What is the difference between traditional and cognitive approaches to New Testament metaphor analysis?
Traditional approaches identify rhetorical figures, while cognitive linguistics examines how metaphors structure conceptual understanding and theological thought.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament corpus linguistics?
To analyze linguistic patterns across large collections of texts using computational methods for statistical analysis.

What is the difference between intuitive and corpus-based approaches to New Testament language study?
Intuitive approaches rely on native speaker intuition and traditional grammar, while corpus-based methods analyze empirical data from actual texts.

What is the primary purpose of New Testament digital humanities?
To apply computational methods and digital tools to biblical texts for new insights in textual analysis, visualization, and interpretation.

What is the difference between traditional philological and digital humanities approaches to New Testament study?
Traditional philology uses manual textual comparison and analysis, while digital humanities employs computational tools for large-scale analysis and visualization.
