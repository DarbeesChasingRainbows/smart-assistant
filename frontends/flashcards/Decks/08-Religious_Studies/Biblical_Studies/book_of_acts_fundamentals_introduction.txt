// Book of Acts Fundamentals & Introduction Deck

What is the primary purpose of the Book of Acts in the New Testament?
To record the birth and expansion of the early church, showing how the Holy Spirit empowered the apostles to spread the gospel from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth.

What is the traditional authorship of the Book of Acts?
Luke the physician, who also wrote the Gospel of Luke, as indicated by the "we" passages, similar style, and dedication to Theophilus.

What is the relationship between the Gospel of Luke and the Book of Acts?
Acts is the sequel to Luke's Gospel, continuing the story of Jesus through the work of the Holy Spirit and the early church.

What is the primary historical context of the Book of Acts?
Written around 80-90 CE, describing events from 30-60 CE, covering the expansion of Christianity from Jerusalem to Rome under Roman rule.

What is the difference between Acts and the Gospels in literary genre?
Acts is historical narrative focusing on church development, while Gospels are biographical narratives focusing on Jesus' life and teachings.

What is the primary purpose of the prologue in Acts 1:1-2?
To connect Acts to Luke's Gospel and establish the continuation of Jesus' ministry through the Holy Spirit and the apostles.

What is the significance of the 40-day period between Jesus' resurrection and ascension?
It provides a transition period for Jesus to prepare his disciples and commission them for their mission.

What is the primary purpose of the Great Commission in Acts 1:8?
To outline the geographical and strategic expansion of the gospel: Jerusalem, Judea, Samaria, and the ends of the earth.

What is the difference between the Great Commission in Matthew 28:19-20 and Acts 1:8?
Matthew emphasizes making disciples and baptizing, while Acts focuses on geographical expansion and Holy Spirit empowerment.

What is the primary significance of Jesus' ascension in Acts 1:9-11?
To mark the transition from Jesus' earthly ministry to the Holy Spirit's ministry through the church.

What is the purpose of the two angels' message at the ascension?
To assure the disciples that Jesus will return in the same way he ascended and to redirect their focus to their mission.

What is the primary purpose of replacing Judas in Acts 1:15-26?
To restore the twelve apostles and fulfill Scripture, preparing for the coming of the Holy Spirit and the church's mission.

What is the difference between Matthias and Paul as apostles?
Matthias was chosen to replace Judas among the twelve, while Paul was later called as the apostle to the Gentiles.

What is the primary significance of Pentecost in Acts 2?
To mark the birth of the church through the outpouring of the Holy Spirit and the first gospel proclamation.

What is the historical and religious context of Pentecost?
A Jewish festival celebrating the first fruits, occurring 50 days after Passover, drawing pilgrims from throughout the diaspora.

What is the primary purpose of the tongues phenomenon in Acts 2?
To demonstrate the Holy Spirit's power and enable the gospel to be understood by people of all languages.

What is the difference between Acts 2 tongues and Corinthian tongues?
Acts 2 tongues were known languages for evangelism, while Corinthian tongues may have been ecstatic speech requiring interpretation.

What is the primary purpose of Peter's Pentecost sermon in Acts 2:14-36?
To explain the significance of Pentecost, proclaim Jesus as Messiah and Lord, and call for repentance and faith.

What is the significance of Joel's prophecy in Peter's sermon?
To show that Pentecost fulfills Old Testament prophecy about the outpouring of the Spirit in the last days.

What is the primary purpose of the early church community description in Acts 2:42-47?
To present the ideal model of Christian community devoted to teaching, fellowship, worship, and evangelism.

What is the significance of the "awe" that came upon every soul in the early church?
It demonstrated God's presence and power through the apostles' signs and the community's radical unity.

What is the primary purpose of the healing in Acts 3:1-10?
To demonstrate the continuing power of Jesus' name through the apostles and validate their gospel message.

What is the significance of healing at the temple gate called Beautiful?
It shows the gospel's power reaching into the heart of Jewish religious life and transforming those marginalized by society.

What is the primary purpose of Peter's second sermon in Acts 3:11-26?
To explain the healing's source in Jesus' power and call for repentance, emphasizing Jesus as the promised prophet.

What is the difference between Peter's first and second sermons in Acts?
The first explains Pentecost and calls for faith, while the second explains healing and emphasizes repentance and restoration.

What is the primary purpose of the Sanhedrin's opposition in Acts 4:1-22?
To show the emerging conflict between the church and Jewish religious authorities and the apostles' bold witness.

What is the significance of the early church's prayer in Acts 4:23-31?
To demonstrate dependence on God amid persecution and to seek boldness for continued witness.

What is the primary purpose of the community sharing in Acts 4:32-37?
To show the radical transformation produced by the gospel in creating generous, sacrificial community.

What is the difference between voluntary sharing in Acts 4 and compulsory communism?
Acts describes voluntary generosity motivated by love, not political or economic coercion.

What is the primary purpose of Ananias and Sapphira's judgment in Acts 5:1-11?
To protect the church's integrity and demonstrate God's seriousness about truth and holiness in the community.

What is the significance of the fear that came upon the church after Ananias and Sapphira?
It showed God's holiness and the importance of integrity in the growing Christian community.

What is the primary purpose of the apostles' continued miracles in Acts 5:12-16?
To validate their message and demonstrate the continuing power of Jesus through the Holy Spirit.

What is the significance of people bringing sick from surrounding towns to Jerusalem?
It shows the growing reputation of the apostles and the spread of the gospel's influence beyond Jerusalem.

What is the primary purpose of the Sanhedrin's second opposition in Acts 5:17-42?
To intensify persecution and test the church's commitment, while showing God's protection and guidance.

What is the significance of Gamaliel's advice to the Sanhedrin?
It provides a pragmatic argument for tolerance and shows how God can work through unexpected circumstances.

What is the primary purpose of the seven's appointment in Acts 6:1-7?
To resolve administrative conflict and allow the apostles to focus on prayer and ministry of the word.

What is the significance of the seven's names and backgrounds?
They represent Hellenistic Jews showing the church's cultural diversity and practical wisdom in leadership.

What is the primary purpose of Stephen's ministry in Acts 6:8-15?
To show the gospel expanding beyond the apostles and the growing opposition leading to the first martyrdom.

What is the significance of Stephen's face being like an angel's?
It demonstrated divine presence and approval of his witness and message.

What is the primary purpose of Stephen's speech in Acts 7?
To show Israel's history of rejecting God's messengers and to proclaim Jesus as the righteous One and Son of Man.

What is the difference between Stephen's speech and previous apostolic preaching?
Stephen provides a comprehensive historical survey culminating in Christ, while others focus more on recent events.

What is the primary purpose of Stephen's martyrdom in Acts 7:54-60?
To show the ultimate cost of discipleship and to model Christ-like forgiveness and faith.

What is the significance of Stephen's vision of Jesus standing at God's right hand?
It confirms Jesus' exaltation and ongoing intercession for his suffering people.

What is the primary purpose of Saul's introduction in Acts 7:58-8:3?
To introduce the primary opponent who would become the primary proponent of the gospel to the Gentiles.

What is the significance of Saul approving Stephen's death?
It establishes his role as persecutor and sets the stage for his dramatic conversion.

What is the primary purpose of the persecution and scattering in Acts 8:1-3?
To show how persecution advances the gospel's spread beyond Jerusalem, fulfilling Jesus' commission.

What is the difference between persecution in Jerusalem and scattering in Judea and Samaria?
Jerusalem persecution concentrated opposition, while scattering dispersed witnesses throughout the region.

What is the primary purpose of Philip's ministry in Samaria in Acts 8:4-25?
To demonstrate the gospel breaking Jewish barriers and reaching Samaritans with power and acceptance.

What is the significance of Simon the Sorcerer's conversion and fall?
It shows the gospel's power to attract and the danger of mixing true faith with selfish motives.

What is the primary purpose of Philip and the Ethiopian in Acts 8:26-40?
To show the gospel reaching Africa and to model personal evangelism and obedience to the Spirit's guidance.

What is the significance of the Ethiopian's baptism and joyful journey?
It demonstrates the gospel's inclusivity and the proper response to understanding Christ.

What is the primary purpose of Saul's conversion in Acts 9:1-19?
To show God's sovereign grace in transforming the church's greatest opponent into its greatest missionary.

What is the difference between Saul's conversion experience and other conversions in Acts?
Saul's involves a dramatic divine appearance, personal encounter with Christ, and immediate commissioning.

What is the primary purpose of Saul's early ministry in Acts 9:20-31?
To demonstrate the transformed life and immediate effectiveness of his new witness, despite initial opposition.

What is the significance of Saul's escape from Damascus and Jerusalem?
It shows the cost of discipleship and God's protection of his chosen instrument.

What is the primary purpose of Peter's ministry in Lydda and Joppa in Acts 9:32-43?
To show the continued apostolic ministry expanding beyond Jerusalem and preparing for the Gentile mission.

What is the significance of Tabitha's (Dorcas) raising?
It demonstrated apostolic power, compassion, and the gospel's impact on practical service and community.

What is the primary purpose of Cornelius's vision in Acts 10:1-8?
To prepare a God-fearing Gentile for the gospel and show God's initiative in reaching all peoples.

What is the significance of Cornelius's character and devotion?
It shows God reaching those who genuinely seek him, regardless of ethnic background.

What is the primary purpose of Peter's vision in Acts 10:9-16?
To overcome Jewish dietary laws and prepare Peter for Gentile inclusion in the church.

What is the difference between Peter's vision and Old Testament food laws?
The vision specifically targets Jewish-Gentile barriers, not general dietary principles.

What is the primary purpose of Peter's visit to Cornelius in Acts 10:17-48?
To mark the official opening of the gospel to Gentiles and demonstrate the Spirit's inclusive work.

What is the significance of the Holy Spirit falling on Gentiles?
It provided undeniable evidence that Gentiles were full members of God's people without becoming Jewish proselytes.

What is the primary purpose of the Jerusalem council's discussion in Acts 11:1-18?
To defend and validate the Gentile mission and overcome Jewish-Christian resistance to inclusion.

What is the significance of Peter's defense at the Jerusalem council?
It shows how personal experience and theological reflection must work together for church unity.

What is the primary purpose of the Antioch church in Acts 11:19-30?
To establish the first multi-ethnic Gentile church and model Christian generosity and mission partnership.

What is the significance of the term "Christian" first used in Antioch?
It shows how the movement developed its distinct identity in a multicultural context.
