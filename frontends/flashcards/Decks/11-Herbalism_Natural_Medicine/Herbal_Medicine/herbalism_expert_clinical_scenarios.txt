Scenario: A 42-year-old female presents with recurrent urinary tract infections (4 episodes in past year), chronic stress, and mild hypertension. She currently takes lisinopril 10mg daily and reports antibiotic resistance concerns. Physical examination reveals mild suprapubic tenderness, and recent urine culture shows E. coli resistant to trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole. She seeks herbal alternatives for prevention and management.

Question: Which THREE herbal actions and considerations are most critical for this patient's comprehensive treatment plan, and what monitoring is essential? (Select ALL that apply)
A) Cranberry proanthocyanidins preventing bacterial adhesion to bladder epithelium
B) Uva ursi arbutin conversion to hydroquinone for antimicrobial urinary tract effects
C) Adaptogenic herbs modulating HPA-axis stress response affecting immune function
D) Diuretic herbs increasing urinary flow for mechanical bacterial clearance
E) ACE-inhibitor herbs potentially interacting with lisinopril causing hypotension
F) Immunomodulating herbs enhancing leukocyte activity and mucosal immunity

Answer: A, C, F
Cranberry proanthocyanidins (A) prevent E. coli adhesion to bladder wall, reducing infection recurrence. Adaptogenic herbs (C) address chronic stress affecting immune competence and UTI susceptibility. Immunomodulating herbs (F) enhance local and systemic immunity for infection prevention. Uva ursi requires acidic urine and has safety concerns, diuretics may worsen hypertension, and ACE-inhibitor herbs could cause dangerous hypotension with lisinopril.
