## üìö Deck Organization & Learning Strategy

### **üéØ Progressive Learning Path (4 Decks)**

#### **Deck 1: Latin Fundamentals & Basic Vocabulary (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Basic grammar, essential vocabulary, foundational concepts
- Latin alphabet, pronunciation, and basic sounds
- Five noun declensions and case functions
- Four verb conjugations and basic tenses
- Essential vocabulary for daily life, family, and common objects
- Basic sentence structure and word order principles

**Learning Outcome**: Master Latin fundamentals and basic reading ability

#### **Deck 2: Latin Grammar & Syntax (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Advanced grammar, complex constructions, syntactical patterns
- Complex verb forms: participles, gerunds, gerundives
- Subjunctive mood and its various uses
- Complex sentence structures: subordinate clauses, indirect statements
- Advanced pronoun usage and agreement
- Rhetorical devices and stylistic elements

**Learning Outcome**: Understand complex Latin grammar and syntax for text analysis

#### **Deck 3: Latin Literature & Advanced Reading (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Classical authors, literary techniques, textual analysis
- Major authors: Cicero, Caesar, Vergil, Ovid, Horace, Catullus
- Poetic meters: dactylic hexameter, elegiac couplets
- Literary genres: epic, lyric, history, philosophy, satire
- Rhetorical devices and literary techniques
- Historical and cultural context of major works

**Learning Outcome**: Read and analyze classical Latin literature with understanding

#### **Deck 4: Latin Advanced Composition & Cultural Context (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Composition skills, cultural understanding, modern applications
- Latin prose and verse composition techniques
- Advanced stylistic analysis and imitation
- Roman culture, history, and social context
- Latin's influence on modern languages and terminology
- Contemporary applications in academia and professional fields

**Learning Outcome**: Compose Latin and understand cultural significance and modern relevance

5. Present analysis with supporting textual evidence
