# Biology Glossary with Pronunciation Guide

## Overview
Comprehensive pronunciation guide for all biological terms found in the Biology deck collection. Organized alphabetically with simplified phonetics and deck references.

## Quick Reference: Commonly Confused Terms
**Mitosis vs Meiosis**: Mitosis (my-TOH-sis) produces identical cells for growth/repair; Meiosis (my-OH-sis) produces reproductive cells with half genetic material
**Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic**: Prokaryotic (proh-KAR-ee-OT-ik) cells lack nucleus; Eukaryotic (yoo-KAR-ee-OT-ik) cells have nucleus and organelles
**Genotype vs Phenotype**: Genotype (JEE-noh-type) is genetic makeup; Phenotype (FEE-noh-type) is observable traits
**Arteries vs Veins**: Arteries (AR-tuh-reez) carry blood away from heart; Veins (vaynz) return blood to heart
**DNA vs RNA**: DNA (DEE-en-AY) stores genetic info; RNA (AR-en-AY) carries out protein synthesis

**Aerobic** (air-OH-bik) - [Cell Biology]
- Requiring oxygen; referring to processes that use oxygen
- Related to: Cellular respiration, metabolism

**Allele** (uh-LEEL) - [Genetics & Heredity]

- Related to: Genotype, phenotype, variation

**Anaerobic** (an-air-OH-bik) - [Cell Biology]
- Not requiring oxygen; referring to processes without oxygen
- Related to: Fermentation, cellular respiration

**Anatomy** (uh-NAT-uh-mee) - [Human Biology Applications]
- Study of body structure and organization
- Related to: Physiology, body systems

**Archaebacteria** (ar-kee-bak-TEER-ee-uh) - [Diversity & Ecology]

- Related to: Prokaryotes, classification

**Arteries** (AR-tuh-reez) - [Human Biology Applications]

## Study Tips

### Memory Strategies
1. **Word Origins**: Many biological terms have Greek/Latin roots (e.g., "prokaryotic" = "before nucleus")
2. **Visual Connections**: Associate terms with diagrams or mental images
3. **Group Similar Terms**: Study related terms together (e.g., all organelles, all systems)
4. **Practice Pronunciation**: Say terms aloud while studying definitions

### Cross-Reference System
- **Cell Biology**: Focus on cellular structures and processes
- **Diversity & Ecology**: Emphasize classification and ecosystem roles
- **Genetics & Heredity**: Concentrate on inheritance mechanisms
- **Human Biology Applications**: Apply knowledge to body systems

### Common Pronunciation Patterns
- "-ic" ending: pronounced "ik" (prokaryot**ic**, eukaryot**ic**)
- "-ology" ending: pronounced "OL-uh-jee" (biolog**y**, physiolog**y**)
- "-plasm" ending: pronounced "plaz-um" (cyto**plasm**, proto**plasm**)
- "-some" ending: pronounced "sohm" (chromo**some**, lyso**some**)

This glossary provides comprehensive pronunciation support for all biology terms across the deck collection, facilitating effective learning and communication in biological studies.
