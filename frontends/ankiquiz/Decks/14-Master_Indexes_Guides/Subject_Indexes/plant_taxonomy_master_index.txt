# Plant Taxonomy Master Index

## Overview
This comprehensive collection contains 150 questions across complete plant taxonomy learning progression: Taxonomy Fundamentals (50 questions), Angiosperm Phylogeny & Classification (50 questions), and Taxonomic Keys & Identification (50 questions). Organized by scientific classification systems with professional taxonomist preparation and integration with field identification collections.

## Certification Alignment
- **Botanical Society of America**: Professional taxonomist certification and research preparation
- **International Association for Plant Taxonomy (IAPT)**: Nomenclature and classification expertise
- **Herbarium Curator Certification**: Specimen management and taxonomic research
- **Botanical Garden Professional**: Collection management and plant classification
- **Environmental Science**: Plant diversity assessment and conservation taxonomy

## Scientific Classification Systems

### APG IV CLASSIFICATION HIERARCHY
**Basal Angiosperms**: Amborellales, Nymphaeales, Austrobaileyales
**Monocots**: Acorales, Alismatales, Pandanales, Liliales, Asparagales
**Commelinid Monocots**: Poales, Arecales, Commelinales, Zingiberales
**Basal Eudicots**: Ranunculales, Proteales, Trochodendrales, Buxales
**Core Eudicots**: Gunnerales, Dilleniales, Saxifragales, Rosids, Asterids

### MAJOR CLADE ORGANIZATION
**Rosids**: Fabids (Fabales, Rosales, Fagales, Cucurbitales, Malpighiales) and Malvids (Brassicales, Crossosomatales, Geraniales, Myrtales, Sapindales)
**Asterids**: Lamiids (Gentianales, Solanales, Lamiales, Boraginales) and Campanulids (Aquifoliales, Asterales, Escalloniales, Bruniales, Apiales, Dipsacales, Paracryphiales)
**Monocots**: Commelinids (Poales, Arecales, Commelinales, Zingiberales) and Non-commelinids (Acorales, Alismatales, Pandanales, Liliales, Asparagales)

## Research Methodology Integration

### MOLECULAR PHYLOGENETICS
**DNA Barcoding**: rbcL, matK, trnH-psbA regions for species identification
**Phylogenomic Analysis**: Target enrichment, transcriptome sequencing, whole-genome approaches
**Molecular Dating**: Relaxed clock methods, fossil calibration, biogeographic analysis

### MORPHOLOGICAL ANALYSIS
**Comparative Morphology**: Vegetative and reproductive character analysis
**Morphometrics**: Statistical shape analysis, geometric morphometrics
**Paleobotanical Integration**: Fossil evidence, evolutionary transitions, temporal context

### INTEGRATIVE TAXONOMY APPROACHES
**Multiple Evidence Lines**: Morphology, genetics, ecology, geography
**Species Delimitation**: Coalescent methods, population genetics, reproductive isolation
**Conservation Applications**: Phylogenetic diversity, evolutionary distinctiveness, prioritization
