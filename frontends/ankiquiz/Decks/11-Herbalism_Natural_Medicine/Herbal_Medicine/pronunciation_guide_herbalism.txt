# Herbalism Pronunciation Guide

## Overview
This guide provides phonetic pronunciation for botanical Latin names, herbal terminology, and preparation methods used throughout the Herbalism collections (Herbalism Fundamentals, Materia Medica, Herbal Preparations, Clinical Applications). Pronunciations use simplified phonetic spelling with emphasis on syllable stress for effective professional communication.

**Anti-inflammatory** (an-tee-in-FLAM-uh-tor-ee) - Reduces inflammation

## Usage Notes

1. **Stress Marks**: Capitalized syllables receive primary stress
2. **Syllable Separation**: Hyphens indicate syllable breaks
3. **Latin Pronunciation**: Follows classical botanical pronunciation standards
4. **Medical Accuracy**: Pronunciations follow standard medical and botanical terminology
5. **Professional Context**: Terms organized for clinical and educational use

## Study Tips

- **Practice**: Say botanical names aloud multiple times for mastery
- **Break Down**: Focus on one syllable at a time for complex Latin names
- **Context**: Learn pronunciations within clinical scenarios
- **Regular Review**: Practice regularly to maintain accuracy
- **Professional Use**: Apply pronunciations in clinical documentation and patient communication
