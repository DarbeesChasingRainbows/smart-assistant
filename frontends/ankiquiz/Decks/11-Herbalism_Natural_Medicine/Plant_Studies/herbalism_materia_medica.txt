// Herbalism - Materia Medica Collection

What is the primary purpose of studying herbal materia medica?
To develop comprehensive knowledge of individual medicinal plants including their properties, actions, constituents, indications, contraindications, and preparation methods for clinical application.

What is the difference between a herbal monograph and materia medica entry?
A monograph provides detailed scientific information including chemistry, pharmacology, and research, while materia medica entries focus on practical clinical applications, traditional uses, and preparation methods.

## IMMUNE SYSTEM HERBS

### Echinacea purpurea (Purple Coneflower)
**Primary Purpose**: Enhance immune function for preventing and treating upper respiratory infections, colds, and flu through immunomodulatory effects on white blood cell activity.

**Key Actions**: Immunostimulant, anti-inflammatory, antiviral, antibacterial, lymphatic tonic

**Active Constituents**: Alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, polysaccharides, flavonoids

**Traditional Uses**: Cold and flu prevention, wound healing, infection treatment, immune support

**Clinical Applications**: Upper respiratory infections, immune enhancement, inflammatory conditions, skin wound healing

**Contraindications**: Autoimmune disorders, immunosuppressant medications, allergic reactions to aster family

**Preparation Methods**: Tincture (1:5, 60% alcohol), decoction of root, capsule of standardized extract

**Dosage Guidelines**: Tincture 2-4 mL three times daily, capsules 300-500 mg standardized extract twice daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe for short-term use (up to 8 weeks), avoid in progressive autoimmune conditions

### Astragalus membranaceus (Huang Qi)
**Primary Purpose**: Support immune function, enhance vitality, and strengthen Wei Qi (defensive energy) for preventing infections and supporting recovery.

**Key Actions**: Immunomodulator, adaptogen, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hepatoprotective

**Active Constituents**: Polysaccharides (astragalans), flavonoids, saponins (astragalosides)

**Traditional Uses**: Chronic immune deficiency, fatigue, adrenal support, post-illness recovery

**Clinical Applications**: Immune enhancement, stress adaptation, cardiovascular support, liver protection

**Contraindications**: Acute infections with fever, autoimmune conditions, pregnancy (high doses)

**Preparation Methods**: Decoction of sliced root, tincture (1:5, 70% alcohol), powder in capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Decoction 9-15 g daily, tincture 3-6 mL three times daily, capsules 500-1000 mg twice daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid in acute febrile conditions, monitor blood pressure in hypertensive patients

## DIGESTIVE SYSTEM HERBS

### Peppermint (Mentha piperita)
**Primary Purpose**: Relieve digestive discomfort, reduce gas and bloating, and soothe gastrointestinal irritation through carminative and antispasmodic actions.

**Key Actions**: Carminative, antispasmodic, aromatic, cholagogue, analgesic

**Active Constituents**: Menthol, menthone, rosmarinic acid, flavonoids

**Traditional Uses**: Indigestion, gas, bloating, nausea, irritable bowel syndrome

**Clinical Applications**: Functional dyspepsia, IBS, tension headaches, respiratory congestion

**Contraindications**: GERD, hiatal hernia, infants under 2, gallbladder disease

**Preparation Methods**: Infusion of leaves, essential oil, tincture (1:5, 45% alcohol)

**Dosage Guidelines**: Infusion 1 cup after meals, essential oil 0.2-0.4 mL in capsules, tincture 2-4 mL three times daily

**Safety Notes**: Avoid in GERD, use enteric-coated capsules for heartburn, caution with gallstones

### Ginger (Zingiber officinale)
**Primary Purpose**: Support digestion, reduce nausea, and provide anti-inflammatory effects through warming carminative and antiemetic properties.

**Key Actions**: Carminative, antiemetic, anti-inflammatory, circulatory stimulant, diaphoretic

**Active Constituents**: Gingerols, shogaols, zingiberene, volatile oils

**Traditional Uses**: Nausea, motion sickness, digestive weakness, colds and flu, arthritis

**Clinical Applications**: Chemotherapy-induced nausea, morning sickness, osteoarthritis, migraine prevention

**Contraindications**: Gallstones, bleeding disorders, high doses in pregnancy, anticoagulant medications

**Preparation Methods**: Fresh ginger decoction, dried powder, tincture (1:5, 60% alcohol)

**Dosage Guidelines**: Fresh 2-4 g daily, powder 1-2 g daily, tincture 2-4 mL three times daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid high doses with anticoagulants, caution with gallstones

## NERVOUS SYSTEM HERBS

### Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)
**Primary Purpose**: Calm nervous system, reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, and relieve tension through nervine and mild sedative actions.

**Key Actions**: Nervine, mild sedative, antidepressant, antispasmodic, carminative

**Active Constituents**: Linalool, linalyl acetate, flavonoids, tannins

**Traditional Uses**: Anxiety, insomnia, nervous tension, headaches, digestive upset

**Clinical Applications**: Anxiety disorders, sleep disturbances, tension headaches, mild depression

**Contraindications**: Sedative medications, pregnancy (high doses), surgical anesthesia (2 weeks prior)

**Preparation Methods**: Infusion of flowers, essential oil, tincture (1:5, 45% alcohol)

**Dosage Guidelines**: Infusion 1-2 cups daily, essential oil 80-160 mg daily, tincture 2-6 mL daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid with sedatives, discontinue before surgery, caution in pregnancy

### St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum)
**Primary Purpose**: Treat mild to moderate depression, anxiety, and nerve pain through effects on neurotransmitters and nerve inflammation reduction.

**Key Actions**: Antidepressant, anxiolytic, antiviral, anti-inflammatory, nerve tonic

**Active Constituents**: Hypericin, hyperforin, flavonoids, essential oils

**Traditional Uses**: Depression, anxiety, nerve pain, wound healing, viral infections

**Clinical Applications**: Major depressive disorder, seasonal affective disorder, neuropathic pain, viral infections

**Contraindications**: Antidepressant medications, photosensitivity conditions, pregnancy, oral contraceptives

**Preparation Methods**: Infusion of flowering tops, oil infusion, standardized extract capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Infusion 2-3 cups daily, standardized extract 300 mg three times daily, oil externally

**Safety Notes**: Significant drug interactions, photosensitivity risk, avoid with MAO inhibitors, SSRIs

## CARDIOVASCULAR HERBS

### Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna)
**Primary Purpose**: Support cardiovascular health, strengthen heart muscle, improve circulation, and regulate blood pressure through cardiac tonic and vasodilatory actions.

**Key Actions**: Cardiac tonic, vasodilator, antioxidant, mild diuretic, astringent

**Active Constituents**: Flavonoids (quercetin, vitexin), oligomeric procyanidins, triterpenoids

**Traditional Uses**: Heart weakness, poor circulation, high blood pressure, angina

**Clinical Applications**: Congestive heart failure, hypertension, angina, atherosclerosis

**Contraindications**: Heart medications (beta blockers, digoxin), hypotension, pregnancy

**Preparation Methods**: Tincture of berries and flowers, decoction, standardized extract capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Tincture 2-4 mL three times daily, standardized extract 300-600 mg twice daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, monitor with heart medications, avoid in severe hypotension

### Garlic (Allium sativum)
**Primary Purpose**: Support cardiovascular health, lower cholesterol, reduce blood pressure, and provide antimicrobial effects through sulfur compounds and circulatory enhancement.

**Key Actions**: Antihypertensive, cholesterol-lowering, antiplatelet, antimicrobial, antioxidant

**Active Constituents**: Allicin, alliin, S-allyl cysteine, flavonoids

**Traditional Uses**: High blood pressure, high cholesterol, infections, immune support

**Clinical Applications**: Hyperlipidemia, hypertension, atherosclerosis prevention, immune enhancement

**Contraindications**: Anticoagulant medications, bleeding disorders, surgery (2 weeks prior)

**Preparation Methods**: Fresh cloves, aged garlic extract, powder capsules, tincture

**Dosage Guidelines**: Fresh 1-2 cloves daily, aged extract 600-1200 mg daily, powder 600-900 mg daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, caution with anticoagulants, stop before surgery, may cause GI irritation

## RESPIRATORY HERBS

### Mullein (Verbascum thapsus)
**Primary Purpose**: Soothe respiratory irritation, reduce cough, and clear lung congestion through demulcent, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory actions.

**Key Actions**: Expectorant, demulcent, anti-inflammatory, antitussive, pulmonary tonic

**Active Constituents**: Mucilage, saponins, flavonoids, iridoid glycosides

**Traditional Uses**: Cough, bronchitis, asthma, respiratory congestion, sore throat

**Clinical Applications**: Chronic bronchitis, dry cough, respiratory irritation, mild asthma

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy (traditional caution), skin contact dermatitis

**Preparation Methods**: Infusion of leaves and flowers, tincture (1:5, 25% alcohol), syrup

**Dosage Guidelines**: Infusion 2-3 cups daily, tincture 2-4 mL three times daily, syrup 1-2 teaspoons

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, strain fine hairs to prevent irritation, caution in pregnancy

### Elecampane (Inula helenium)
**Primary Purpose**: Treat chronic respiratory conditions, clear mucus, and strengthen lung tissue through expectorant, antibacterial, and lung tonic actions.

**Key Actions**: Expectorant, antibacterial, antitussive, pulmonary tonic, diaphoretic

**Active Constituents**: Inulin, alantolactone, essential oils, sesquiterpene lactones

**Traditional Uses**: Chronic bronchitis, asthma, whooping cough, respiratory weakness

**Clinical Applications**: Chronic respiratory infections, bronchitis with mucus, weakened lung tissue

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy, allergy to aster family, high blood pressure

**Preparation Methods**: Decoction of root, tincture (1:5, 60% alcohol), syrup

**Dosage Guidelines**: Decoction 1 cup 2-3 times daily, tincture 2-3 mL three times daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid in pregnancy, caution with aster family allergies

## ENDOCRINE/HORMONAL HERBS

### Vitex (Vitex agnus-castus)
**Primary Purpose**: Regulate female hormones, balance menstrual cycles, and treat PMS and menopausal symptoms through pituitary gland modulation and progesterone support.

**Key Actions**: Hormone regulator, progesterone precursor, prolactin inhibitor, uterine tonic

**Active Constituents**: Iridoid glycosides (agnuside), flavonoids, essential oils

**Traditional Uses**: Menstrual irregularities, PMS, infertility, menopause, lactation issues

**Clinical Applications**: Premenstrual syndrome, menstrual irregularities, menopausal symptoms, infertility

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy, hormone-sensitive cancers, antipsychotic medications, oral contraceptives

**Preparation Methods**: Tincture of berries, capsules of powdered berries, standardized extract

**Dosage Guidelines**: Tincture 1-2 mL morning, capsules 400-500 mg daily, standardized extract 20-40 mg daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid in pregnancy, may interfere with contraceptives, gradual onset

### Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa)
**Primary Purpose**: Treat menopausal symptoms, menstrual cramps, and arthritis through estrogenic effects, anti-inflammatory actions, and uterine muscle relaxation.

**Key Actions**: Estrogenic, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, analgesic, hypotensive

**Active Constituents**: Triterpene glycosides, phenolic acids, alkaloids, flavonoids

**Traditional Uses**: Menopause, menstrual cramps, arthritis, nerve pain, childbirth

**Clinical Applications**: Menopausal hot flashes, menstrual pain, rheumatoid arthritis, nerve pain

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy, liver disease, hormone-sensitive cancers, anticoagulant medications

**Preparation Methods**: Tincture of root, decoction, standardized extract capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Tincture 2-4 mL three times daily, standardized extract 20-40 mg twice daily

**Safety Notes**: Monitor liver function, avoid in pregnancy, caution with hormone-sensitive conditions

## MUSCULOSKELETAL HERBS

### Turmeric (Curcuma longa)
**Primary Purpose**: Reduce inflammation, treat arthritis, support joint health, and provide antioxidant protection through curcumin compounds and anti-inflammatory actions.

**Key Actions**: Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, analgesic, hepatoprotective, circulatory stimulant

**Active Constituents**: Curcuminoids (curcumin), demethoxycurcumin, volatile oils, starch

**Traditional Uses**: Arthritis, inflammation, digestive disorders, skin conditions, wound healing

**Clinical Applications**: Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, inflammatory conditions, digestive support

**Contraindications**: Gallstones, bleeding disorders, anticoagulant medications, surgery (2 weeks prior)

**Preparation Methods**: Powder in capsules, tincture (1:5, 60% alcohol), standardized extract

**Dosage Guidelines**: Powder 1-3 g daily, standardized extract 400-600 mg three times daily, tincture 2-4 mL

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, enhance absorption with black pepper, caution with gallstones, stop before surgery

### Willow Bark (Salix alba)
**Primary Purpose**: Reduce pain and inflammation, treat headaches and arthritis, and lower fever through salicin compounds and anti-inflammatory actions.

**Key Actions**: Analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, astringent, rheumatic remedy

**Active Constituents**: Salicin, salicortin, flavonoids, tannins

**Traditional Uses**: Headaches, arthritis, fever, pain, rheumatic conditions

**Clinical Applications**: Low back pain, osteoarthritis, headaches, fever reduction

**Contraindications**: Aspirin allergy, children under 16, pregnancy, anticoagulant medications

**Preparation Methods**: Decoction of bark, tincture (1:5, 25% alcohol), capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Decoction 1 cup 2-3 times daily, tincture 2-4 mL three times daily, capsules 240-480 mg

**Safety Notes**: Avoid in children, aspirin allergies, pregnancy, caution with anticoagulants

## SKIN/EXTERNAL HERBS

### Calendula (Calendula officinalis)
**Primary Purpose**: Promote wound healing, reduce skin inflammation, treat skin infections, and soothe irritated skin through anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and tissue regenerative actions.

**Key Actions**: Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, wound healer, lymphatic, skin tonic

**Active Constituents**: Triterpenoids, flavonoids, carotenoids, essential oils

**Traditional Uses**: Wounds, burns, rashes, skin infections, inflammation

**Clinical Applications**: Wound healing, dermatitis, minor burns, skin infections, inflammation

**Contraindications**: Allergy to aster family, deep wounds requiring medical attention

**Preparation Methods**: Infused oil, cream/ointment, tincture (1:5, 45% alcohol), compress

**Dosage Guidelines**: External application 2-3 times daily, oil for massage, cream for localized treatment

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe externally, avoid internal use during pregnancy, allergy caution

### Tea Tree (Melaleuca alternifolia)
**Primary Purpose**: Treat skin infections, reduce inflammation, and provide antimicrobial effects through terpene compounds and broad-spectrum antimicrobial actions.

**Key Actions**: Antimicrobial, antifungal, antiviral, anti-inflammatory, antiseptic

**Active Constituents**: Terpinen-4-ol, cineole, terpinolene, alpha-terpineol

**Traditional Uses**: Skin infections, fungal conditions, acne, wounds, inflammation

**Clinical Applications**: Acne, fungal infections, minor wounds, skin inflammation, dandruff

**Contraindications**: Internal use, pregnancy (high doses), severe skin conditions

**Preparation Methods**: Essential oil, cream/ointment, diluted oil, wash

**Dosage Guidelines**: External 5-10% solution, cream 2-3 times daily, oil diluted 1:10 for sensitive skin

**Safety Notes**: External use only, patch test first, avoid internal use, keep away from eyes

## ADAPTOGENS

### Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)
**Primary Purpose**: Reduce stress, enhance vitality, improve sleep, and support immune function through adaptogenic, nervine, and rejuvenative actions.

**Key Actions**: Adaptogen, nervine, immunomodulator, anti-inflammatory, rejuvenative

**Active Constituents**: Withanolides, alkaloids, amino acids, fatty acids

**Traditional Uses**: Stress, fatigue, anxiety, immune weakness, rejuvenation

**Clinical Applications**: Stress adaptation, anxiety, insomnia, immune enhancement, cognitive function

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy, hyperthyroidism, sedative medications, autoimmune disorders

**Preparation Methods**: Powder of root, tincture (1:5, 50% alcohol), standardized extract capsules

**Dosage Guidelines**: Powder 3-6 g daily, tincture 2-4 mL twice daily, standardized extract 300-600 mg twice daily

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid in pregnancy, monitor thyroid levels, caution with sedatives

### Rhodiola (Rhodiola rosea)
**Primary Purpose**: Combat fatigue, enhance mental performance, reduce stress, and support physical endurance through adaptogenic and stimulating actions.

**Key Actions**: Adaptogen, antidepressant, cognitive enhancer, anti-fatigue, cardioprotective

**Active Constituents**: Rosavins, salidroside, tyrosol, flavonoids

**Traditional Uses**: Fatigue, depression, cognitive enhancement, physical performance, altitude sickness

**Clinical Applications**: Stress fatigue, depression, cognitive function, exercise performance

**Contraindications**: Bipolar disorder, anxiety disorders, stimulant medications, pregnancy

**Preparation Methods**: Tincture of root, standardized extract capsules, powder

**Dosage Guidelines**: Tincture 1-2 mL twice daily, standardized extract 100-300 mg twice daily, powder 200-600 mg

**Safety Notes**: Generally safe, avoid in bipolar disorder, caution with stimulants, start with low dose
