## üìö Deck Organization & Learning Strategy

### **üéØ Progressive Learning Path (4 Decks)**

#### **Deck 1: Basic Chemistry Fundamentals & Atomic Structure (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Atomic theory, periodic table, chemical bonding, foundational concepts
- Atomic structure: protons, neutrons, electrons, isotopes, ions
- Periodic table organization: elements, groups, periods, trends
- Chemical bonding: ionic, covalent, metallic, molecular geometry
- Chemical formulas and equations: writing, balancing, interpreting
- States of matter: solids, liquids, gases, phase transitions

**Learning Outcome**: Master chemistry fundamentals and basic atomic/molecular understanding

#### **Deck 2: Basic Chemistry Chemical Reactions & Stoichiometry (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Reaction types, quantitative relationships, kinetics, equilibrium
- Reaction types: synthesis, decomposition, single/double replacement, combustion
- Stoichiometry: mole calculations, limiting reactants, percent yield
- Chemical kinetics: reaction rates, collision theory, catalysts
- Chemical equilibrium: equilibrium constants, Le Chatelier's principle
- Thermochemistry: enthalpy, entropy, Gibbs free energy, spontaneity

**Learning Outcome**: Understand quantitative relationships and reaction behavior

#### **Deck 3: Basic Chemistry States of Matter & Solutions (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Gas laws, solution chemistry, colligative properties, phase behavior
- Gas laws: Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, combined gas law, ideal gas law
- Solution chemistry: solubility, concentration units, saturation
- Colligative properties: boiling point elevation, freezing point depression
- Acids and bases: pH, neutralization, buffers, titration
- Phase transitions: melting, boiling, sublimation, phase diagrams

**Learning Outcome**: Master physical behavior of matter and solution properties

#### **Deck 4: Basic Chemistry Laboratory Techniques & Applications (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Laboratory safety, analytical methods, real-world applications
- Laboratory safety: PPE, MSDS, emergency procedures, hazard identification
- Analytical techniques: titration, spectroscopy, chromatography, calorimetry
- Laboratory equipment: glassware, balances, measurement techniques
- Real-world applications: environmental, medical, industrial chemistry
- Scientific methods: experimental design, data analysis, scientific communication

**Learning Outcome**: Apply chemistry knowledge in laboratory and career contexts
