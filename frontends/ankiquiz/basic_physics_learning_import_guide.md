## üìö Deck Organization & Learning Strategy

### **üéØ Progressive Learning Path (4 Decks)**

#### **Deck 1: Basic Physics Fundamentals & Mechanics (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Classical mechanics, forces, motion, energy, and fluids
- Kinematics: position, velocity, acceleration, motion equations
- Dynamics: Newton's laws, forces, friction, gravity, momentum
- Energy: work, power, kinetic and potential energy, conservation laws
- Rotational motion: torque, angular momentum, rotational dynamics
- Fluids: pressure, buoyancy, flow, viscosity, surface tension

**Learning Outcome**: Master fundamental physics principles and mathematical problem-solving

#### **Deck 2: Basic Physics Waves, Optics & Thermodynamics (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Wave phenomena, light, heat, and energy transformations
- Waves: mechanical and electromagnetic waves, sound, interference
- Optics: reflection, refraction, lenses, diffraction, polarization
- Thermodynamics: heat, temperature, laws of thermodynamics, heat engines
- Heat transfer: conduction, convection, radiation, thermal expansion
- Phase changes: latent heat, phase diagrams, material properties

**Learning Outcome**: Understand wave behavior, optical systems, and thermal processes

#### **Deck 3: Basic Physics Electricity & Magnetism (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Electric charges, currents, magnetic fields, and electromagnetic phenomena
- Electrostatics: charge, Coulomb's law, electric fields, potential
- Circuits: current, resistance, Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's laws, power
- Magnetism: magnetic fields, forces on moving charges, magnetic materials
- Electromagnetism: induction, Faraday's law, transformers, AC circuits
- Electromagnetic waves: spectrum, radiation, antennas, Maxwell's equations

**Learning Outcome**: Master electrical and magnetic principles for modern technology

#### **Deck 4: Basic Physics Modern Physics & Applications (100 cards)**
**Focus**: Quantum mechanics, relativity, nuclear physics, and contemporary applications
- Quantum mechanics: wave functions, uncertainty principle, quantum numbers
- Relativity: special relativity, time dilation, length contraction, E=mc¬≤
- Nuclear physics: radioactivity, fission, fusion, nuclear energy
- Applications: medical physics, renewable energy, quantum computing, nanotechnology
- Particle physics: Standard Model, fundamental forces, particle accelerators

**Learning Outcome**: Understand modern physics and cutting-edge technological applications
